Design Principles

Warm & Approachable

Warm ivory base (#FAF8F3) instead of stark white. Soft clay and warm taupe tones create an inviting feel while maintaining sophistication. Not distant or cold — welcoming but refined.

Quiet Luxury

Low-saturation colors, refined restraint. Nothing loud, everything intentional. Sage green and soft clay accents add personality without overwhelming.

Generous Whitespace

Editorial magazine feel with lots of breathing room. Sections should feel spacious, never cramped. Line-height at 1.7-1.8 for body text.

Minimal Formatting

Use prose, not bullet points. Small caps for section labels. Subtle hover states with the accent color. No garish transitions or effects.

Grain & Texture

Subtle film grain overlay on images (3-5% opacity) for that fashion photography aesthetic. Adds warmth without being obvious.

Technical Precision

Clean code, fast loading, accessible. The design should feel effortless but the implementation should be pristine. CS major who cares about craft.

This style guide defines the visual language for a portfolio that balances technical expertise with design sensibility.
